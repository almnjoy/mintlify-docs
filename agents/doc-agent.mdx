# Doc Agent

The Doc Agent runs in `#solar-info` and is the educational layer of the TrueNorth AI platform. It answers technical and general questions about solar energy — designed for homeowners who are still learning.

## What It Does

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Solar Education">
    Explains how solar panels work, what components make up a system, how grid-tie and net metering function, and what realistic expectations for output look like in Canadian climates.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Component Questions">
    Covers panels, inverters (string, micro, power optimizer), racking, monitoring systems, and battery storage. Helps customers understand trade-offs.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Permits and Incentives">
    Explains the permitting process, utility interconnection requirements, and available federal and provincial solar incentives (CEIP, provincial grants, net metering programs by province).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Industry Terminology">
    Defines kW vs kWh, payback period, degradation rate, PV, DC/AC ratio, and other terms customers encounter during the sales process.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Persona

```yaml
personality: solar-doc
system_prompt: >
  You are a solar documentation expert helping with permits, utility
  interconnection paperwork, HOA approvals, and incentive documentation.
```

## Channel Flow

The Doc Agent frequently redirects warm, interested prospects to `#solar-sales` to connect with the Sales Agent. This handoff is intentional — education first, sales second.

<Tip>
  The Doc Agent links directly to the [Solar 101](/solar/solar-101) and [Install Process](/solar/install-process) pages in these docs when customers want to read more.
</Tip>
